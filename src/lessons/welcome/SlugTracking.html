Slug Tracking

After all this excitation of slug hunting, we will have to learn hot to track their trail. Your buggle just happened to found the trail of a slug: a green dribbling tracks. If it manage to follow it to its end, it will find a baggle representing an appetizing slug (from a buggle point of view).

To reach that goal, you had to write the previously given boolean method isFacingTrail, which should determine whether we are facing a green cell or not. Of course, if we are facing a wall, it should return false. It would be great if this method could have no side effect. on the calling buggle and on the world.

Remember that this method takes the color of the trail we want to follow as an argument. Slugs leave green trails, but other prey leave trails of other colors. In Java, there is a data type Color to indicate the colors. The green color is defined by Color.green.

Exercise goal

Complete the isFacingTrail() method (which gets called by hunt() automatically). You don't have to write the hunt() method.
